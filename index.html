Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam(15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015) usually known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was a famous Indian aerospace engineer. He was the 11th President of India, from 2002 to 2007. When he was president, he was popularly known as the People's President. He was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour in 1997. He has also been a professor (of aerospace engineering). Kalam is the first Chancellor of the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology Thiruvananthapuram (IIST). He is called as the "Missile Man" of India.

Achievements of APJ Abdul Kalam!!

A prominent aerospace engineer, Dr. Kalam was a key figure in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, and the successful launch of the satellite Rohini in 1980. His leadership at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) significantly contributed to the country's advancements in space technology and missile systems. Dr. Kalam's achievements extend beyond the realm of science and technology; he was an eloquent speaker, a prolific author, and a passionate advocate for education. He believed in empowering the youth and frequently interacted with students, encouraging them to dream big and work towards realizing their goals. His books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," continue to inspire millions. Even after his presidency, Dr. Abdul Kalam remained an influential figure, focusing on education and societal development. His legacy lives on, and he is remembered not only for his remarkable contributions to science and technology but also for his inspiring leadership and commitment to shaping a better future for India. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, leaving behind a legacy of inspiration and dedication to the service of the nation.
